Quietman posted 08-05-98 06:44 PM CT (US)            
For me at least, the novelty of these color variations has totally worn off. I won't spend another cent on these questionable color varations.

Months ago, I was intrigued by the previous set of 4 holiday variations, so I bought the red snowman and the black skull. And I still think the black skull looks very cool.

And then there were the neon psych eyes, and I actually bought one of those too. I don't think it looks cool -- it's the ugliest PEZ I ever saw. I bought it just in case it becomes valuable one day.

But now, it seems like the floodgates have been opened, and these "special" offers are going to become EXTREMELY commonplace, effective immediately. They're just going to keep pumping out color variations, as long as there are collectors who keep shelling out $20+ a pop to get that coveted chartreuse snowman.

Forget it -- I'm out. I think they're going to keep releasing these color oddities as long as they can make lots of money (which means, they'll keep doing it for a while yet). By which point, everyone will be so sick of the color variations that NONE of them will be worth anything.

So if you really like the way they look, and if you have $20 burning a hole in your pocket, don't let me stand in your way. But if you think these color variations will ever be worth anything, as collectibles, I bet you're wrong.

And the thing is, how is ANYBODY supposed to know whether any given production run is really, truly limited to "3000" or whatever number the dealers claim? There's simply no way to validate those numbers. I'm spending my money on other things, thanks anyway.

Quietman posted 08-06-98 08:25 AM CT (US)            
Re: Michael's advice to not buy PEZ "as an investment".... I think I know what he's saying, but I would phrase it differently: I'd say, don't buy PEZ *solely* as an investment.

I enjoy collecting PEZ, esp. the new issues that I can buy retail. But, when it comes to buying older PEZzes, I've sometimes paid over $50 apiece, like when I bought my Zorro dispenser. I simply couldn't justify spending $50+ on a PEZ dispenser if there wasn't some element of it being an "investment."

Not so much investment as in, "Oh boy, I'll get rich off of this!" More like, "Yeah, it's a lot of money for a little piece of plastic... but if I need the cash, I should be able to sell it for at least as much as I paid for it." So for me, it's both a hobby and an investment.

But I have to admit, the black skull looks much, much cooler than the regular skull. I'd love to buy a black witch to go with him... and I would buy it if it cost, say, $5.

Quietman posted 08-16-98 11:35 AM CT (US)            
I think the PEZ collectors of the world have already started to tire of these color variations. Or at least, they're tired of paying upwards of $15 apiece for them.

The evidence: The latest batch of color variations don't seem to be fetching very high prices on eBay. I just saw auctions for the lion and pink elephant, which were still bidding at around $11.00, MOC. (And that was after the auction had been going for almost 7 days.) By comparison, the original set of color varations seemed to sell pretty quickly at $25.00 apiece MOC.

This decline in interest from collectors is perfectly natural. When the first batch of holiday variations came out, I'm sure a lot of people--myself included--thought, "Wow, these could be gone in no time, I'd better buy some." But here we are, however many months later, and the original color variations are still readily available... and already there's another round of variations. I guess it's obvious, now, that these things won't fly off the shelves overnight.

And I'm sure that the dealers realize this. Note that SJGlew's web page hasn't been offering the new set of color variations for very long, but they're already offering the selling them at a discount -- 2 for $30 as an alternative to $20 apiece.

I still want that black witch, but I think I'll wait till it's under $10.
